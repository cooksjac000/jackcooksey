G.R.I.E.V.O.U.S
Robotics Research Lab

In the Fall 2025 semester, I have been working as a research assistant in the Autonomous Systems Group. There, I'm working with my PI, Alexander Nettekoven, to develop a low-cost household robot. This robot, G.R.I.E.V.O.U.S., is designed to assist with household chores and other menial tasks. So far, I've built and programmed Grievous to work in teleop, and now we're moving on to training an AI model with our data.

January-April 2025

Comparator
Combat Robotics Project

In Spring 2025, I led my combat robotics team, the Mechaniacs, in building a 3lb combat robot to compete in the Texas Robo Rumble. TRR was a tournament-style competition hosted by UT, where 15 teams competed with a $300 budget each. Over an 11-week build phase, I led all aspects of creating the robot, from brainstorming to final iterations. Our robot, Comparator, placed 5th overall in the competition, 1st among teams with no 3lb experience.

June-July 2025

ONA
International Barcelona Internship

Over the Summer of 2025, I was a software development intern at Vaive Logistics, a robotics startup in Barcelona, Spain. Vaive's goal is to develop an autonomous delivery vehicle, ONA, that will replace human drivers in last-mile delivery, saving money and reducing carbon emissions. At Vaive, I developed the user control pipeline to enable user to robot control and robot to user feedback.

Fall 2022-Spring 2024

9105 Technotalons Team Leader
First Robotics Competition

I was a founding member and team captain of the First Robotics Competition team 9105 Technotalons. There, I led a team of more than 30 kids in building competitive robots for yearly competitions. I mentored my team in all sorts of engineering skills, all the way from CAD and wiring design to assembling the robot and machining using CNC machines. Under my leadership, the team competed in the world championships one year and the state championships both years.
